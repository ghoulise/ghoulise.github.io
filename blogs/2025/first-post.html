Hi everyone! After thinking it was a good idea to be deliberate in my actions with respect to my goals, I thought that clearly defining these goals are also an important step.

2026 New Years Resolutions

  1. Achieve Expert or Master on Kaggle - This one is really important to me as I wish to rapidly improve my data analysis skills and Kaggle preforms tight feedback loops that help you actually hone in on key skills
  2. Get Accepted into a Masters Program - Necessary to keep chasing my academic goals.
  3. Publish a paper or two in ML or Math - Same as above, really important for actually increasing my profile and making sure I can be competitive, also researching is fun, this incentivies tight feedback.
  4. 4.0 GPA Spring 2026 & Fall 2026 - Very improtant to make sure I'm understanding material and reflects in my records.
  5. Get an Internship - Really want to improve my coding skills and the best way to do this and to improve my resume is to get a coding job.
  6. Contribute to 1 MLSys Open-Source Project There are a lot of nice open source projects looking for volunteers in EleutherAI and GPU Mode. My goal is to hopefully get my name out there and be helpful to atleast one project.
  7. Set up CUDA-capable work environment - This has held me back, I think I should either try to ask my school's HPC or figure out a way to SSH into the free online alternatives. Important to be able to upskill in MLSys.
  8. Write semi-consistent biweekly technical blogs - I wish to keep this page active and to blog my progress, especially with research orientated tasks. This is a good way to keep on track and keep progress metrizable.

Some of these are particularly ambitious, but I believe if I push myself I should be able to achieve most of these. Thanks for stopping by and reading some of the goals I've set for myself this year.
